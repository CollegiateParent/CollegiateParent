Enrolled at a VT college?
We're here to help.
Whether you’re an out-of-state student or a Vermont resident, we can help you cover 2nd semester costs.

2nd semester bills are due soon. VSAC can assist you.

Although learning may look a little different this year as schools and training programs continue to adjust their plans, VSAC's commitment to Vermont students remains unchanged.

All students enrolled in college at a Vermont institution (and their parents) have access to the services of Vermont's nonprofit higher education agency. That includes helping families pay for undergraduate or graduate education.

3 ways we can help:
At VSAC, we're all about making sure you're receiving any free aid you can and helping you minimize your loan costs. Know more. Borrow less.
  1. Financial aid resources (including free aid). Have you applied for all "gift" aid for which you're eligible? This includes the FAFSA, grants from your home state, scholarships, and free college aid. All Vermont residents should apply for a VT grant. 
  2. Student loan education and support. The reality is that many families need to borrow to pay some of their college costs. Our Vermont-based customer service team can help you understand your loan options so you borrow only what you need and reduce your cost of borrowing.
  3. Low-cost fixed-rate loans. Our parent and student loans for undergraduate or graduate/professional studies offer fixed rates as low as 3.99% APR: our lowest fixed rate ever. Apply if you are from out of state and enrolled at a Vermont college or are a VT resident.

Get to know us at vsac.org or reach out to our Vermont team at 800-226-1029 from 8 am-4:30 pm, or email info@vsac.org.

VSAC | Helping families save, plan & pay for college & career training.
The Vermont Student Assistance Corp. was created in 1965 as a nonprofit, state-based organization to provide families with the information and financial resources they need to save, plan and pay for education after high school. VSAC administers Vermont grants and scholarships and sponsors the Vermont 529 college savings plan, with a state income tax credit on annual contributions for Vermont taxpayers. VSAC also offers the Vermont Advantage loans for parents and students to help families finance the costs of undergraduate and graduate education.
